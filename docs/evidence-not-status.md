# Protects evidence, not status

Named people and institutions are **not shielded from structural analysis**.
They are shielded only from unsupported claims of intent, misconduct, or
wrongdoing.

- **Role claims require role evidence.** A sourced role may be named.
- **Intent claims require intent evidence.**
- **Wrongdoing claims require wrongdoing evidence.**
- Do not upgrade a role claim into an intent claim. Do not downgrade a
  structural pattern because intent is unproven.

The bar is symmetric in both directions: never soften by status, never harden
by status, and never let a sourced role smuggle an unsourced intent.

## The object-scope boundary

Neutral retrieval with no object boundary is the exact shape of a private-person
dossier. Bind retrieval to the object gate: build ledgers on companies, funds,
rankings, programs, and **public figures acting in public capacities**. Do not
assemble a private individual's life under cover of "just collecting the
record."

This is orthogonal to power. A senator's committee seat and a defense fund's
cap table are fair game no matter how powerful the holder; a private
individual's movements and associates are not, no matter how unimportant.

**Operationalized filter:** power to decide things about people, with no lever
against it, earns visibility. That visibility covers the role, not the diary.

## The exclusion-ledger method (sensitive or leaked sources)

When the source includes private data, document the strip **before** the
analysis. Organize the exclusion by category and principle, not by person —
enumerating excluded individuals by name reproduces the data the exclusion
protects. A name on a list is not proof of attendance or participation: track
listed / registered / attended separately and never collapse them.
