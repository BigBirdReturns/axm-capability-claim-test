# Field Card (v2)

The method the tool enforces, in working form.

## Step 0. Object gate (run first, always)

| Object | What you are actually testing | Route |
|---|---|---|
| Product company | Does the capability work | Operating proof + contamination |
| Capital allocator | Is the claimed judgment independently attributable | Attribution proof + contamination |
| Integrator / platform | Does the mission survive removal of the vendor | Removal test + operating control + contamination |
| Ranking / validator / media | Is the legitimacy it confers independent | Independence proof + contamination |
| Government program / vehicle | Who owns the seams | Removal test + ownership |
| Claim only | — | Tense, source class, baseline, beneficiary |

## Step 1. Sourcing gate

No verdict below three sourced load-bearing fields. Return a pull-list instead.

## Step 2. Posture

Forensic analyst, not chaperone, not prosecutor.

1. Falsifiable both ways — state what would clear it, every time.
2. Baseline before verdict.
3. Classify every claim: confirmed, reported, derived, judgment, open. Never
   upgrade beyond the source.
4. Structural, not behavioral.
5. No manufactured culprit.
6. Symmetry — operationalize every soft word: sovereign, proven, deployed,
   partner, validated, ranked, selected.

## Step 3. The whole test in one line

Separate **survivability proof** (capital, valuation, marquee investors, board
names, demos, awards, rankings, "deployed," press) from **operating proof**
(competed contracts and programs of record, independently verified performance
against a stated baseline, a named operational customer, reproducible results).
Then ask how independent the survivability proof is.

## Step 4. Two axes

- **Axis 1 — Operating proof:** A / B / C / unclassifiable, loop open or closed.
- **Axis 2 — Capital contamination:** clean / mixed / circular.

Read the cell: A/Clean real; A/Circular probably real, read the contracts;
B/Clean early but arms-length; B/Circular unproven and self-validated;
C/Circular investment vehicle in a capability costume.

## Step 5. The seams (product company)

1. Capital-to-contract gap. 2. Tense of proof. 3. Metric substitution.
4. Marketing minus doctrine. 5. Independent customer. 6. Credibility broker and
consortium. 7. Self-referential legitimacy. 8. Lineage as proof. 9. Removal test
on capability. 10. Ownership of seams.

**Capital allocator** swaps Step 5 for attribution: platform vs personal vs
in-the-room attribution of each claimed win, whose capital created the outcome,
whether selection preceded social validation, what founder-visible help is
independently tied to them.

Each seam carries one formal state: `triggered`, `not_triggered`, `unclear`,
`not_applicable`. **`not_applicable` is never counted as weak signal.**

## Step 6. Contamination

A bucket, with reasons, never a bare number. See `contamination.md`.

## Step 7. Trace to roots

Name each layer; at each, ask who supplies the belief and who retains the value.
Name the propagation mechanism (usually capacity asymmetry and compatible
incentives — no conspiracy required).

## Step 8. The verdict

Object/route · sourcing status · operating proof (A/B/C, loop) · contamination
bucket with components · largest gap (quantified) · tense of proof · evidence
table · **what would clear it** (mandatory) · roots · next pulls.

## The two ways models fail

- **The chaperone** defers to a powerful subject and narrates caution.
- **The prosecutor** assumes the costume is empty and reaches for fraud.

Land between them: full speed on the evidence, full discipline on the claim.
